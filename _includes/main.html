Publications

PowerFi: Turning Billions of Wi-Fi Devices Into Ambient Power Sources
Ning An, Ruirong Huang, Renjie Zhao
Under Review

PowerFi is a novel wireless power transfer system that leverages ubiquitous Wi-Fi infrastructure to provide ambient power for IoT devices. By exploiting the physical layer characteristics of Wi-Fi signals, PowerFi can efficiently harvest energy from Wi-Fi transmissions without interfering with normal data communication. This work demonstrates the feasibility of turning billions of existing Wi-Fi devices into power sources, enabling battery-free operation for low-power IoT applications.

UniSlice: Unified and Adaptive Scheduling for Efficient Inference in Heterogeneous GPU Clusters
Zijun Zhu, Ning An, Haokai Gong, Ruiting Zhou, Wenxiao Liu, Wentao Liu, Kun Tian, Wei Wang, Renwei Li and Liang Du
Under Review

Efficient inference over heterogeneous GPU clusters requires jointly optimizing spatial resource partitioning and temporal batching under dynamic workloads, which constitutes a challenging online optimization problem. Existing systems are tightly coupled to vendor-specific slicing mechanisms and rely on rigid SLO-driven heuristics, limiting scalability and adaptability across diverse hardware platforms. In this paper, we formulate heterogeneous GPU scheduling as a joint spatio‚Äìtemporal allocation and batching optimization problem aimed at minimizing average job completion time (JCT). We design UniSlice, a unified and adaptive scheduling framework that implements a low-overhead online greedy algorithm based on coarse-grained latency prediction. UniSlice introduces a vendor-agnostic resource abstraction that unifies heterogeneous sharing mechanisms, and a hierarchical control architecture combining global slice allocation with decentralized adaptive batching. This design enables fast scheduling decisions with theoretical constant-factor performance guarantees while remaining robust to prediction errors. We implement UniSlice on heterogeneous clusters of up to 32 cards using NVIDIA MIG/MPS and Huawei AVI GPUs and conduct extensive evaluations under balanced and imbalanced workloads. Experimental results show that UniSlice reduces average JCT by up to 77.7\% and decreases SLO violation rates by over 50\% compared with state-of-the-art baselines, while achieving microsecond-level scheduling overhead and strong scalability.

CTx: A Concurrent Transmitter for Heterogeneous IoT Communication
Shuai Wang, Ning An, Shuai Wang, Weiwei Chen, Xianjun Deng
In submission

CTx presents a novel concurrent transmission mechanism for heterogeneous IoT communication. By exploiting physical layer concurrent transmission techniques, CTx enables a single Wi-Fi transmitter to simultaneously communicate with multiple heterogeneous IoT devices using different protocols (e.g., Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, ZigBee) without hardware modifications. This work significantly improves the efficiency and flexibility of IoT communication systems.

Hierarchical Spatiotemporal Attention Network for Fine-grained Brain Cognitive State Recognition
Yike Wu, Ning An, Zixuan Zeng, Youyong Kong
ICASSP, 2025 | [abs] | [bib] |

Brain cognitive state recognition based on functional Magnetic Resonance Imaging(fMRI) can capture brain functional activities under different tasks and help understand the neural mechanisms of the brain, which has always been one of the focuses of neuroscience research. Different from the prediction of the brain cognitive domain, the prediction of brain fine-grained cognitive state is based on each moment in the process of executing the task. Therefore, it is necessary to extract more fine-grained effective information. Existing studies focus on modeling and classifying the complete time series, ignoring the brain activity state at each time. So we propose a hierarchical spatiotemporal attention network (FineBrainNet) to recognize fine-grained brain cognitive state. Guided by coarse-grained cognitive domain labels, we trained different sub-modules for fine-grained states under each cognitive domain to capture relevant cognitive state changes more accurately in specific task. Extensive experiments on the HCP-Task dataset show that FineBrainNet can achieve accurate prediction of fine-grained brain cognitive state.
